About Me

I am a PhD student in the computer science department at Rutgers University in Piscataway, New Jersey. I work in the Center for Computational Biomedicine Imaging and Modeling (CBIM) under the supervision of Dimitris Metaxas. My main areas of research include computer vision, pattern recognition/machine learning/data mining, and artificial intelligence. Specifically, my research focuses on developing semantically-grounded, context-driven, and explanation-based mathematical models for complex data analysis. My main application focus is on scene understanding: analyzing real world images of scenes using computational methods. I received a bachelor's degree in computer science and engineering from Lehigh University in Bethlehem, Pennsylvania and minored in cognitive science. While at Lehigh, I worked with a number of faculty including Henry Baird, Jeff Heflin, and Brian Chen. I also participated in REUs under the supervision of Mubarak Shah (University of Central Florida) and Saâd Biaz (Auburn University).
